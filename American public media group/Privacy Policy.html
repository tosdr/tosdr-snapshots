  • Site usage information – Details regarding how and when you use this Site, including the device used to connect to this Site, your internet protocol (IP) address, the frequency and duration of your visits, and information about your interaction with this Site.

  • Survey and demographic information - Demographic details, such as age, education status, profession, income level, and gender may be collected if a member participates in our surveys or audience research, or otherwise responds to a request for such information from APMG.

  • Job applicant information – If you apply for a job through this Site, contact information, information regarding your qualifications and background, educational information, and any other information you provide as part of your application or the application process.

OTHER INFORMATION WE MAY COLLECT

Like most websites, we use various technologies to automatically collect information about you, including the following:

  • IP Address or Device Identifier – When you visit this Site, we collect your Internet Protocol (“IP”) address, which identifies the computer, device, or service provider that you use to access our website.  We may use these identifiers to collect information about the length of time spent on our website or the specific areas visited.

  • Cookies - Cookies are small text files that can be used by websites to make a user’s experience more customized.  The cookies used in connection with this Site may be deployed by us or one of our third-party vendors or service providers (described in more detail in the section titled, “Third Party Cookies,” below). This Site uses different types of cookies to, among other things, automatically recognize you when you return to this Site, store information regarding your preferences (including preferred payment methods), serve relevant advertising to you, personalize content for you, provide social media functionality, and analyze our website traffic and audience. Cookies may collect, transmit or store information that does not reference any other personal information, such as usage data, or cookies may collect, transmit, or store information that does reference other personal information, such as name and credit card information. You may have the option to set your browser, application, or device to accept all cookies, reject all cookies, notify you when a cookie is set, or delete cookies. Please note that if cookies are disabled, you may not be able to enjoy certain features of this Site.

  • Web beacons and other technologies – This Site may use other tracking tools, including web beacons, which are small electronic images embedded in web content and email messages that are not ordinarily visible to users.  Web beacons allow us to track pages and content accessed and viewed by users, as well as to monitor email readership.  We may also use pixels to track visitors. 

  • GPS and location tracking - We may also collect precise, real-time location information from your mobile device in connection with your use of our mobile applications. We may use that information to deliver notifications to you via our mobile applications.  We may also link location information to information that identifies you. Your mobile device may allow you to adjust your settings so that location information is not available to any mobile website or application. If you have questions about the security and privacy settings of your mobile or tablet device, please refer to instructions from your mobile service provider, the Google Play Store or Apple App Store, or the manufacturer of your device.

The information we receive through these types of technologies may depend on your browser or device settings. The information we receive from your web browser and device may not, in and of itself, be personally identifiable. Generally, we use the information described above in the aggregate to help us improve this Site and make it more compatible with the technology used by our visitors. However, we may combine it with other information in an attempt to identify you (in which case it becomes personal information), or we may combine it with personal information. We may also review our server logs for security purposes—for example, to detect intrusions into our network—and we might share our server logs, which contain visitors’ IP addresses, with the appropriate investigative authorities who could use that information to trace and identify you.
