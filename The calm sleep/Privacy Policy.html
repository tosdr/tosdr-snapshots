CCPA Privacy Rights (Including Non-sale of Personal Information)

As a website that functions in multiple jurisdictions, we serve and also comply with the California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018 (CCPA) for natural persons residing in the State of California, and this statement explains the rights of California consumers under the CCPA.
Under the CCPA, among other rights, California consumers have the right to:
“Right to Disclosure” “Right to Access” “Right to be Forgotten” Access to the “Do Not Sell My Information” option “Right to Opt-Out of Marketing” “Right to Fair Treatment”- Request that a business that collects consumer’s personal data disclose the categories and the specific pieces of personal data that a business has collected about consumers.
- Request that a business delete any personal data about the consumer that a business has collected.
- Request that a business that sells consumers' personal data, not sell the consumer's personal data.
As per the above rights granted to you by California law, we duly comply with the requirements. As per the CCPA, ‘Personal Information’ is the information that “identifies, relates to, describes, is capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular consumer or household.” Though, personal information does not include publicly accessible, identity-less, or aggregate consumer information (which are all defined under the CCPA). Sleep Alora explicitly establishes the definition of Personal Information under the purview of CCPA as enunciated in subsection (o)(1) of 1798.140. Note: In line with universal data protection standards, we strictly do not negligently appropriate the information we collect (explicitly defined in this document, and on the Cookies Policy page as well). If in the future, we decide to process or transmit this information in the form of a sale service, we shall comply with the legal obligations and make necessary changes to this statement., and more importantly shall take your permission to conduct such activities.
If you make a request, we have one month to respond to you. If you would like to exercise any of these rights, please contact us. In line with the compliance as per CCPA, we shall update this Privacy Policy page every 12 months. For the purpose of compliance with the California Privacy Law, we have made the due disclosures in the above section, ‘Usage of Information’. Subject to certain limitations and exceptions, the CCPA provides California consumers with the right to request to know more details about the categories and specific pieces of personal information, to delete their personal information, to opt out of any “sales” that may be occurring, and to not be discriminated against for exercising these rights.
