As is standard practice, Digital ReLab may also log non-personally identifying information, including IP address, profile information, aggregate user data, browser type, and device type from the Website and through the Services. User IP addresses are recorded for security and monitoring purposes.

In addition, Digital ReLab uses technological measures, such as pixel tags – tiny graphic images – to tell us what parts of the Website or Services have been visited or to measure the effectiveness of searches users perform. These technological measures also enable us to send email messages in a format that users can read, and they tell us whether emails have been opened to assure that we are only sending messages that are of interest to our users.

Digital ReLab may also use a user’s email address to send updates, newsletters, or news regarding the Services. Digital ReLab may send email messages which use a “click-through URL” linked to content on the Website and Services. When a user clicks one of these URLs, they pass through our web server before arriving at the destination web page. Digital ReLab may track this click-through data to help determine interest in particular topics and measure the effectiveness of our user communications. If you prefer not to be tracked you may simply avoid clicking the text or graphic links in the email. Users who no longer wish to receive promotional messages or updates may do so by opting-out. To opt-out, click the “unsubscribe” link in the email or reply with the word “unsubscribe” in the subject line of the email. 

We use the information that we collect to develop, deliver, and improve our Website, Services, and other offerings. In cases where you use a mobile device to access the Website and use the Services, we may receive information about the location of your device.
