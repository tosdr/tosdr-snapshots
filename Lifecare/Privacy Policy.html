THE INFORMATION WE COLLECT

We may collect two basic types of information: Personal Information ( as defined in this policy) and Anonymous Information (as defined in this policy).

Personal Information – In connection with services, you or your employer or sponsoring organization (“Sponsor Organization”) must provide certain information so we can identify you and provide you services as you requested. Examples of such data include:

  • Registration Information – Information such as your first name, last name and email address and in some cases, Member ID or Company Registration code are collected during registration. In some cases, this information is provided to LifeCare directly by you or your Sponsor Organization.LifeCare also collects a secret question which you will use for online password resets or identifying you when calling in for assistance. Once logged in, you may decide to provide other optional data types such as home address(s), phone #(s) to assist with transactions (described below). In some cases, you have the ability to extend your benefits to family members at which point we collect information such as the family member’s first name, last name, gender and the relationship to you.
  • Transactional Information – In order to fulfill your request for services (i.e. information, referrals, services and/or discounts in a region you specify) we may need to collect additional information such as residence address and supporting details surrounding your particular need for the service. This maybe acquired Chat, Email, or a call depending on your Sponsor Organization’s plan, we may be required to collect credit card information to fulfill services. LifeCare’s systems do not store or transmit credit card information, however we partner with the trusted third party as specified in the “ How We Share Your Information ” section.
  • Public or Community Forums – In some cases, your Sponsor Organization may subscribe to a “classifieds” feature allowing you to post or purchase personal items for sale with other Members. In such cases, you would enter data types such as item description, pricing, pictures and contact information you wish to provide.
  • Information Received from Third Parties – LifeCare may receive your Personal Information from third parties in connection with services. We categorize these sources as LifeCare Sourced and Affiliated Third parties.
  • LifeCare Sourced – In order to provide you with the best web and communication experience, LifeCare utilizes several third parties to provide us information on how you use our website, how our website is performing, and the effectiveness of our email communication so we can continue to customize and improve your experience. This is in form of cookies placed on your computer. Please see the “Cookies” under the “ How We Collect Information ” section for more information.
  • Affiliated Third Parties – LifeCare’s services maybe offered to you through a service you have with another service provider. As part of your agreement with them, you have agreed to provide information to LifeCare. In such cases, your name and email address will be collected by LifeCare.

Anonymous Information – means information that does not identify you personally, but can provide us with usage data, either individually or in the aggregate. Non-Identifiable Information may include demographic information, aggregated information, certain information collected automatically through your device such as web browser information, server log files, cookie technology, and other technologies, and other non-personally identifiable information collected by us or provided by you.

HOW WE COLLECT INFORMATION

We may collect information from you in the following ways:

By Request : We may collect some information from you by asking that you provide it to us.

Cookies – We may use cookies on the LifeCare Sites to allow us to, among other things, measure activity and to personalize your experience. “Cookies” are small text files that reside on your computer to identify you as a unique user. For instance, cookies may enable us to remember your viewing preferences without requiring you to re-type a user-name and password. Cookies may also allow us to track your status or progress when requesting products, services, or information. Specifically, LifeCare leverages session cookies. These are not permanent cookies and are removed from when your browser closes or your computer is powered off. LifeCare also utilizes persistent cookies to enhance your experience with our site. If you choose, you can set your browser to reject cookies or you can manually delete individual or all of the cookies on your computer by following your browser’s help file directions. However, if your browser is set to reject cookies or you manually delete cookies, you may have some trouble accessing and using some of the pages and features that are currently on our website, or that we may put on our website in the future. To take full advantage of the www.lifecare.com experience, we recommend that you leave them turned on.

Pixels – A tracking pixel is an HTML code snippet which is loaded when a user opens an email. It is useful for tracking user behaviors to help us gage the effectiveness of our communications.

Web Server Logs/IP Address – The computers that deliver our web pages to you may record information. For example, a web server log may record the search terms you enter on our websites, the link you clicked to bring you to the web page, your IP address, or cookies that are set on your browser.

We maintain reasonable administrative, technical and physical safeguards designed to protect the Personal Information that you provide.
