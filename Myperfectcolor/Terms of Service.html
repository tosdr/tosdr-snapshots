    Digital Color Spaces

    Digital color spaces such as RGB, Hex, and CMYK are not reliable ways to specify paint colors. These color spaces are device dependent and will yield different colors based on the measurement device used. You also cannot extract these values from an image or any online representation and expect paint to match. Any other color space values derived from these digital color spaces will carry their inherent inaccuracy.

    MyPerfectColor can recreate paint colors based on these digital color spaces, but they are calculated matches that cannot be verified against a physical source as the actual colors only live in the digital domain. MyPerfectColor would input the color values into our matching software and generate a formula which would be used to make paint. There is no way to know what the physical representation of this color should be and nothing physical to compare against. Moreover, a color on a digital display cannot be compared to a physical color. It isn’t possible as the mind cannot simultaneously compare an illuminated color to a physical color.

    There is far too much variation to expect matching colors using digital color spaces to yield accurate results. If you are trying to reproduce a physical color, then the only way to do this accurately is by matching paint against the actual physical part, swatch or sample.

    Any colors ordered using digital color values are at the buyer’s own risk and are sold As Is with no refunds, returns or exchanges. All sales are final. We warn customers of this before purchasing and the paint is custom made to order per the buyer’s instructions.
