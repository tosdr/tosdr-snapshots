California Online Privacy Protection Act
CalOPPA is the first state law in the nation to require commercial websites and online services to post a privacy policy. COPPA (Children Online Privacy Protection Act)

When it comes to the collection of personal information from children under the age of 13 years old, the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) puts parents in control. The Federal Trade Commission, United States’ consumer protection agency, enforces the COPPA Rule, which spells out what operators of websites and online services must do to protect children’s privacy and safety online.

GDPR Compliance
Article 5 of the GDPR contains six principles by which all personal data must be processed. Petscams abides by this process by ensuring the following:
1. Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency: obey the law; Petscams.com only processes personal data in a way that users would reasonably expect; always being open about its data protection practices.
2. Purpose limitation: Petscams.com only processes personal data for the reasons stipulated and nothing else.
3. Data minimization: Data collected pertains to each individual post entry and only collects data that the user has publicly provided and consented to providing.
4. Accuracy: petscams.com makes sure sure that any personal data held is adequate and accurate.
5. Storage limitation: Petscams.com stores data pertaining to each individual post for the extent of its listing. Once a post has been removed, all data pertaining to the users that have commented on this post will then be securely disposed of.
6. Integrity and confidentiality: Data is processed securely and confidentially. Data will never be sold to third parties. However, we do reserve the right for third parties to access data for the purpose of anonymous surveying and research by law enforcement and academics.
