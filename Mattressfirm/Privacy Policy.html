You may disable some of these tracking tools by managing your cookie preferences on your internet browser or by enabling the Global Privacy Control signal (which we recognize) through your browser; however, if you choose to use our website, you expressly consent to the use of those tracking tools, including performance and functional cookies, that are necessary for the proper functioning of our websites and cannot be disabled.


  • Clickstream Data.  We collect clickstream data, which provides a non-personalized record of your activity on the internet using the items and webpages that you click on.  We use this data to analyze how you use our websites.
  • Cookies.  We collect cookies, which are small text files that may be stored on your device when you use our websites.  The cookies we use may contain information – such as a unique user ID – or collect information, such as your IP address or if you create an account with us on any of our websites, email address.  Cookies allow us to recognize your device when you return to our website. They also help us to understand how you use our website and to provide you with advertising and content that we think may be of interest to you.
  • Pixel Tags.  A pixel tag (also known as a web beacon) is a small string of code that represents a clear graphic image.  We use pixels to collect non-personalized information about your activity on our website (e.g., time, date, duration, and number of page views), so that we can improve our website functioning and to provide you with advertising and content that we think may be of interest to you.

The tags may collect information about your activity on our websites and may be used by these third parties to provide us with data and marketing analytics, to assist us with creating or effecting advertising campaigns, and to assist with order fulfillment. Our Privacy Policy does not cover these third-party companies. Please contact these third-party companies (e.g., Google and PayPal) directly for more information about their privacy policy and your choices regarding the tags and the information collected by the tags.

I. About This Privacy Policy
This Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy applies to consumer health data that is collected in the State of Washington with specific rights regarding consumer health data as defined by the Washington My Health My Data Act (the "WMHMDA”). Specifically, it describes the practices of Mattress Firm, Inc. (“Mattress Firm,” “us,” “we,” or “our”) in its capacity as a regulated entity, that link to this Consumer Health Data Policy regarding the collection, use, disclosure, or sale of consumer health data that we collect from Washington consumers (“Washington consumers”, “you”, or “your”). This Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy supplements the Mattress Firm Privacy Policy and applies solely to Washington consumers as such term is defined under the WMHMDA.

II. Categories of Consumer Health Data Collected
Pursuant to the WMHMDA, Mattress Firm may collect and use your consumer health data, which is defined as personal information that is linked or reasonably linked to a consumer and that identifies the consumer’s past, present, or future physical or mental health status. Specifically, Mattress Firm may collect the following categories of consumer health data from you:

  • Bodily functions, vital signs, symptoms, or measurements of the information described in subsection (8)(b) of the WMHMDA.

    • Through your use of the Sleep.com mobile application, we collect measurements of your respiration, movement, noise disturbances, and other sleep measurement data (collectively, “Sleep Data”).

    • Via the Sleep.com mobile application, we request permission to read and write health data from the Health app. Thereby allowing the importing of SERVICE sleep parameters into the Heath app. Your Apple® device will only allow Mattress Firm, via the Sleep.com mobile application, to read and write such data if you grant permission via your device’s settings.
    • For users of Google Health Connect, Mattress Firm, via the Sleep.com mobile application, requests permission to read health data from Google Health Connect. Thereby allowing the importing of SERVICE sleep parameters into the Health app. Your Health Connect device will only allow Mattress Firm, via the Sleep.com mobile application, to read such data if you grant permission via your device’s settings.

  • Individual health conditions, treatment, diseases, or diagnosis.
    • Through your use of the MattressMatcher® web application, we ask to what extent (e.g., often, sometimes, never) your sleep is impacted by sleep disruptors such as pain, allergies, snoring, sleep apnea and acid reflux.

  • Any information that a regulated entity or a small business, or their respective processor, processes to associate or identify a consumer with the data described in Section 19.373.010 (8)(b)(i) through (xii) of the WMHMDA that is derived or extrapolated from non-health information (such as proxy, derivative, inferred, or emergent data by any means, including algorithms or machine learning).
    • We collect your daily SleepScore®, an assessment of the quality and quantity of your sleep plotted on scale of 0 to 100, that is generated by the Sleep.com mobile application through your use of the Sleep.com mobile application.

III. Why We Collect and Use Your Consumer Health Data
We collect and use this consumer health data to the extent necessary to provide you with the products and services that you have requested from us (such provision, collectively, the “Services”). The Services include delivering to you, as applicable, the Sleep.com mobile application and the MattressMatcher® web application, and providing you with insights and education required to achieve greater understanding of your sleep condition. Such insights may include, but are not limited to, personalized product and service recommendations based on the consumer health data that you provide. Examples of why we collect your consumer health data include:

  • To enable you to track your Sleep Data including sleep duration, sleep onset, sleep staging and other collected information to provide you with a SleepScore® and other key metrics that allow you to take actionable steps to better sleep. Access to your Sleep Data is required for the Sleep.com mobile application to work. 
  • To supplement your Sleep Data to improve our ability to provide personalized sleep analysis and recommendations. 
  • To provide you with product and service recommendations, including matching you to the recommended mattress via the MattressMatcher® web application.

IV. Categories of Sources from Which Consumer Health Data is Collected
We may collect this consumer health data directly from you, as applicable, via the Sleep.com mobile application and the MattressMatcher® web application and when you elect to show any of our Sleep Experts the contents of your Sleep.com mobile application, as well as from the following sources via the Sleep.com mobile application:
