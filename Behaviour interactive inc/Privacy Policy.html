    Welcome to the Behaviour Interactive privacy policy (“Policy”).  This Policy was developed because we respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your privacy.

    Summary of the Policy

     

    What Personal Data is collected by BHVR?

    • Profile and Identity data (e.g. internal identifier);
    • Technical data (e.g. IP address);
    • Contact data (e.g. email address), and all other Personal Data detailed at section 2.1 below.

     

    What purposes are the Personal Data used for?

    • To provide and operate the Services, incl. for the purposes of providing online leaderboards, matchmaking, and friends search;
    • To deliver relevant content and personalized/targeted advertisements;
    • To analyze and provide statistics of the use of the Services;
    • To manage clearly unlawful conduct and misuse of the Services;
    • To deliver personalized newsletter, and promotions;
    • To process job applications;
    • For other purposes detailed at section 3.1.

     

    Is the Personal Data shared with third parties?

    We may engage subcontractors to collect and process your Personal Data on our behalf and for our benefit, in order to provide certain services such as sending e-mails and processing payments. These subcontractors may be located in various countries, including outside the European Union, UK, Canada, and USA. When the Personal Data is shared with our subcontractors, we take adequate contractual measures to protect the Personal Data. See section 6 for more information.

     

    1. Scope and Services

     

    1.1 Scope of Policy

    This Policy applies when you use any of the Services (as defined below) offered by Behaviour Interactive Inc. or any of its affiliates (collectively referred to as “we”, “our” or “BHVR”).

    The terms of this Policy (together with our end-user license agreement (EULA) and terms of use (available here https://www.bhvr.com/behaviour-tou/ applies to your use of the Services.  In certain circumstances, we may provide you with other privacy-related notice. This Policy supplements such other notices.

     

    1.2 Definition of Services

    This Policy aims to give you information on how we collect, use, process and disclose Personal Data that you provide us through your use of our websites and apps, our games and products, BHVR account, social media channels, customer and technical support and any other service BHVR provides, which may be hosted on video game digital distribution services, or downloaded or streamed onto your device (the “Services”).

     

    2. Personal Data Collected

     

    2.1 Type of Personal Data

    We may collect, use, store, and disclose different kinds of personal data about you, as follows:

    • Contact Data: delivery address, email address and telephone numbers.
    • Content Data: includes information stored on your device, including friends’ list, and login information.
    • Employment Data: includes any personal information you provide or which you attach to your application when you apply for one of our available positions.
    • Identity Data: names, usernames or similar identifiers (e.g. identification codes of platform used on your device or used for in-game currencies), date of birth, gender.
    • Marketing/Communications Data: includes your preferences in receiving marketing from us and our third parties and your communication preferences.
    • Profile Data: includes your username and password, purchase history, your interests, preferences, feedback, contests, survey responses, information you provide when contacting our support services.
    • Technical Data: includes internet protocol (IP) address, login data, browser information & plug-ins, time zone, operating system and platform, and other technology on the devices you use to access our Services.
    • Transaction Data: includes details about payments to and from you and details of purchases.
    • Usage Data: includes information and preference about how you use our Services (for example your game history, your score and your level).

    (collectively referred to as “Personal Data”).

    We may also collect, use, store, and disclose Aggregated Data such as statistical or demographic data for any purpose. Aggregated Data could be derived from your Personal Data but is not considered Personal Data as this data will not (directly or indirectly) reveal your identity.

    We do not collect any Special Categories of Personal Data about you (including details about your race or ethnicity, religious, political or philosophical beliefs, sexual orientation, health information).

     

    2.2 If you fail to provide Personal Data

    Where we need to collect Personal Data by law, or under the terms of a contract we have with you, and you fail to provide that data when requested, we may not be able to perform the contract we have or are trying to enter into with you (for example, as necessary to be able to provide you with the Services). In this case, we may have to cancel the Services and/or you may be unable to use certain Services or features.

    4. How is Your Personal Data Collected

    We use different methods to collect Personal Data including through:

     

    4.1 Direct interactions

    You may give us your Identity Data when you:

    1. Apply for and/or use our Services, including subscriptions to publications, newsletters and downloading or registering for a Service (or when you become beta tester, influencer or playtester);
    2. Create an account (or profile) for our Services, including when you download patches and demos;
    3. Enter a contest, event or survey or create/provide content as a user of our Services;
    4. Make purchases (online stores or virtual items in games), or request marketing information be sent to you, including personalized or targeted ads content;

    4.2 Data Available Publicly & Social Media

    You may provide Personal Data when you publish it on public areas of our Services or transmit it to other users of the Services or to third parties (collectively, “User Contributions”), as part of games, online forums, blogs, discussion platforms, public user profiles or other similar forums on our Services, such as chats (“Forums“). The data you disclose in the Forums becomes public data and may be read or used by other users of the Services. We cannot control the actions of other users with whom you may choose to share your User Contributions. As such, if you disclose Personal Data on the Forums, you do so at your own risk and we are not responsible for the use of such Personal Data by third parties.

     

    4.3 Automated Technologies, Cookies, Analytics Metric Tools and Other Technologies

    As you use our Services, we may automatically collect Personal Data about your equipment, browsing actions and patterns or Personal Data (incl. Usage Data). We collect these data by using cookies and other similar technologies. We may also use these technologies to collect information about your online activities over time and across third-party websites or other online services (behavioural tracking).  We use cookies and/or other tracking technologies to distinguish you from other users of the Services and to remember your preferences. This helps us to provide you with a good experience when you use the Services and also allows us to improve the Services.  You can set your browser to refuse all or some browser cookies, or to alert you when websites set or access cookies. If you disable or refuse cookies, please note that some parts of the Services may become inaccessible or not function properly. Our Services may also integrate ad serving technologies, including for personalized ads, from third party suppliers (including, but not limited to, UnityAds) that may use pixels and/or other similar technologies for these purposes. Data collected by these third parties may include, without limitation, (i) your age and/or gender, (ii) your IP address and/or country and/or city, (iii) the advertisements placed and their location in the Services or the game, (iv) the duration of visibility of an advertisement as well as the number of views, (v) the identifiers and settings of your device, (vi) your use of the Services, products, and/or other websites (including third party websites) you have viewed (as well as the date and the time of such viewing), and/or (vii) your responses to the advertisements, if any, to allow our suppliers to calculate the number of views of the advertisements or to present ads in a format that best suits your device.

    We are not responsible for circumvention of any privacy settings or security measures contained on our Services Sites.  BHVR declines all liability if the Personal Data is subject to unauthorized use or access.

    This privacy section for California Residents (“CA Privacy Section”) supplements the information contained in other sections of this Policy. This CA Privacy Section applies solely to all visitors, users, and/or State of California residents (“consumers” or “you“). We adopt this notice to comply with the California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018 (CCPA) and any terms defined in the CCPA have the same meaning when used in this CA Privacy Section.  Please note that in other sections of the Policy, you should consider the expression “personal data” to mean “personal information”.

    15.1       Personal Information We Collect

    While providing the Services, BHVR collects information that identifies, relates to, describes, references, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular consumer, household, or device (“personal information“). Personal information does not include:

    • Publicly available information from government records.
    • Deidentified or aggregated consumer information.

    BHVR may collect the following categories of personal information about California residents:

    1. Identifiers such as real name, postal address, unique personal identifier, online identifier, Internet Protocol address, email address, account name, or other similar identifiers.
    2. Personal information categories listed in the California Customer Records statute (Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.80(e)), such as name, address, telephone number, employment, employment history.
    3. Protected classification characteristics under California or federal law, such as gender or date of birth.
    4. Commercial information such as records of products or services purchased, obtained, or considered, or other purchasing or consuming histories or tendencies.
    5. Biometric information, which may include Sensitive Personal Information used to uniquely identify you.
    6. Internet or other similar network activity.
    7. Sensory data, such as audio, electronic, visual information.
    8. Professional or employment-related information, such as current or past job history.
    9. Non-public education information (per the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (20 U.S.C. Section 1232g, 34 C.F.R. Part 99)), such as education records directly related to a student maintained by an educational institution or party acting on its behalf, such as grades, transcripts.
    10. Inferences drawn from any of the personal information identified above.

    15.5       Your Rights & Choices

    The CCPA provides consumers with specific rights regarding their personal information. This section describes your CCPA rights and explains how to exercise those rights.

    15.5.1 Right to Know and Data Portability: You have the right to request that we disclose certain information to you about our collection and use of your personal information over the past 12 months.

    15.5.2 Right to Delete: You have the right to request that we delete any of your personal information or sensitive personal information that we collected from you and retained, subject to certain exceptions. Please note that your request to delete data may impact your use of the Services in some cases, and we may decline to delete information for reasons set forth in this CA Privacy Section or as permitted by the CCPA. We will delete or deidentify personal information and/or sensitive personal information not subject to one of these exceptions from our records and will direct our service providers to take similar action.

    15.5.3 Right to correct inaccurate personal information: If you believe that personal information we maintain about you is inaccurate, you have the right to request that we correct that information.

    15.5.4 Right to opt-out of sharing personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising purposes: California residents have the right to opt out of the sale of their personal information and sharing of personal information for cross-contextual behavioural advertising. If you would like to opt out of sale of your personal information and sharing of personal information for cross contextual behavioural advertising for Tracking Technologies, including cookies and pixels, you will need to click here and opt out of cookies by using the “Cookie Consent Tool” for each BHVR website you visit. We also accept certain opt-out preference signals sent from your browser that will opt you out of the sale and sharing of your personal information. Depending on how you have interacted with our services and our ability to identify you, this may only opt you out of sales and sharing that take place via cookies.

    15.5.5 Right to restrict sensitive personal information processing: You have the right to limit our use and disclosure of Sensitive Personal Information to uses that are reasonably necessary to perform services, provide goods to you, or as otherwise permitted by CCPA.

    15.5.6 Personal Information Sales or Sharing Opt-Out Rights and Sensitive Personal Information Use Limitation: If you are age 16 or older, you have the right to direct us to opt-out as per below:

    A. to not sell or share your personal information and sensitive personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising purposes at any time. Note that we do not sell the personal information or sensitive personal information of consumers we actually know are less than 16 years old. Consumers who opt-in to personal information sales may opt-out of future sales or sharing at any time. and/or

    B. to limit the use of not sell your sensitive personal information at any time. Note that we do not use the sensitive personal information of consumers we actually know are less than 16 years old.

    15.5.7 Right to Non-Discrimination: A California consumer has the right not to receive discriminatory treatment by a business for the exercise of their privacy rights conferred by the CCPA.

    15.5.8 Exercising Your Rights

    A. General

    If you, or someone legally authorized to act on your behalf, wish to exercise your rights, please submit a request to know, delete, correct, opt-out, or restrict processing related to your personal information:

    • by clicking here: https://support.bhvr.com; or

    You may only submit a request to know twice within a 12-month period. Your request to know, delete, correct, opt-out, or restrict processing must: 1) provide sufficient information that allows us to reasonably verify you are the person about whom we collected personal information or an authorized representative; and 2) describe your request with sufficient detail that allows us to properly understand, evaluate, and respond to it.

If we determine that the request warrants a fee, we will tell you why we made that decision and provide you with a cost estimate before completing your request.

However, you may change your mind and opt back in to personal information sharing or use of sensitive personal information at any time by managing your preferences as you see fit, using link below.
