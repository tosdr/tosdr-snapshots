Kissmetrics’s Privacy Policy

EFFECTIVE DATE: DECEMBER 28, 2023

As used in this Privacy Policy, Sandstorm Analytics refers to all websites (including https://www.kissmetrics.io, https://app.kissmetrics.io), properties, and services owned or operated by Sandstorm Analytics, Inc., aka Kissmetrics.

This Privacy Policy is part of Sandstorm Analytics Terms of Use and describes the privacy practices of Sandstorm Analytics. It explains when and how Sandstorm Analytics collects end user and client information, including Personally Identifiable Information (defined below), how we use such information, and the circumstances under which we may disclose such information to others. This Privacy Policy includes the policies that Sandstorm Analytics observes for compliance with laws in the United States as well as other applicable laws, including those followed regarding the transfer of Personally Identifiable Information from the European Economic Area (EEA) and/or Switzerland to the United States under the EU-U.S. Privacy Shield and Swiss-U.S. Privacy Shield, respectively.

By visiting the Sandstorm Analytics website, visiting a Sandstorm Analytics client website that provides notice of its use of Sandstorm Analytics and/or this policy, or registering for our service, you acknowledge that you accept and consent to the practices outlined in this Privacy Policy.


What Types of User Information Does Sandstorm Analytics Obtain?

Visitors to the Sandstorm Analytics Website. If you are a visitor to the Sandstorm Analytics website or a Sandstorm Analytics client (Client) who registers or signs into the Sandstorm Analytics website (including for purposes of obtaining a free trial of Sandstorm Analytics services), we may obtain the following types of information from you or concerning your computer or device (Sandstorm Analytics Client Information), which may include information that can itself be used to identify and/or contact you (Personally Identifiable Information):

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Website name and URL
  • Credit card number
  • Billing address
  • Visitor site preferences

Sandstorm Analytics may also receive certain information that may be provided by your browser or mobile device, including:

  • Browser information
  • Operating system information
  • Mobile device information (e.g., device identifier, mobile operating system, etc.)
  • IP address
  • Pages accessed
  • Time of visit
  • Time of last visit
  • Referring site, application, or service, including the relevant search queries that led you to Sandstorm Analytics website

Visitors to the Sandstorm Analytics Blog. If you wish to access certain functions within the Sandstorm Analytics Blog, such as posting comments or registering for webinars, we may ask you to provide information, including:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Your website

Visitors to Sandstorm Analytics Clients Websites. Our Clients may use customized and proprietary Sandstorm Analytics code, software and/or services (Service(s)) to obtain information regarding the activities that users engage in while visiting their web pages (Client User Data). When a user visits a website that uses Sandstorm Analytics Service, Sandstorm Analytics code contained on the website contacts Sandstorm Analytics servers and enables Sandstorm Analytics to collect Client User Data. Some Clients may also use their own code to obtain Client User Data and then send such information to Sandstorm Analytics servers. Client User Data may include Personally Identifiable Information, including, but not limited to, the types of information listed for Sandstorm Analytics Client Information above.

Client User Data is stored on Sandstorm Analytics web servers and databases (including those hosted on third party servers on behalf of Sandstorm Analytics) for use in performing analysis and producing reports for the applicable Sandstorm Analytics Client. In addition, Sandstorm Analytics itself uses the Sandstorm Analytics Service in order to obtain insights into visitors activities on its own websites, which include, as of the Effective Date, https://www.kissmetrics.io and https://www.kissmetrics.io/blog.

Sandstorm Analytics collection and analysis of Client User Data helps our Clients to evaluate and understand how their users are interacting with their websites, and potentially, to modify their websites in order to make them more valuable or useful for their users. Sandstorm Analytics Clients determine the types of Client User Data that are sent to Sandstorm Analytics servers for analysis. This data may include or be linked to Personally Identifiable Information depending on how the Client uses Sandstorm Analytics Services on its website(s). Sandstorm Analytics Clients are contractually prohibited from sending personally sensitive Client User Data (e.g. personal health information, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs) to Sandstorm Analytics and Sandstorm Analytics does not knowingly receive such data.

In addition, Sandstorm Analytics may collect certain information about a Clients visitors computer that may be provided by the visitors browser. This includes browser information, operating system information, mobile device information, IP address, time of visit, and the referring site, application, or service. Such information may be collected and shared with the Sandstorm AnalyticsClient even though not specifically requested by the Client.

How Do We Use This Information?

Visitors to the Sandstorm Analytics Website. We use the Sandstorm Analytics Client Information, including Personally Identifiable Information, to register your account, fulfill your requests, understand how you are engaging with Sandstorm Analytics, communicate with you, determine if you are satisfied with the Service, process billing for Sandstorm Analytics Services, and personalize your experience on the Sandstorm Analytics website and with the Sandstorm Analytics Service.

We may also use Sandstorm Analytics User Information to facilitate the delivery of content or advertisements that we believe may be of interest to you, or to communicate with you about new or existing products and services or special offers. For more information on opting out of these communications, please see the section on User Privacy Controls below.

Visitors to the Sandstorm Analytics Blog. We may use information you voluntarily provide when visiting the Sandstorm Analytics blog to send you e-mail communications, such as information on upcoming webinars, and to understand how you are engaging with and perceive Sandstorm Analytics Services.

Visitors to Sandstorm Analytics Clients Websites. Except as described in the Disclosure of Information to Third Parties section below, Sandstorm Analytics never sells or shares any Client User Data with individuals or companies other than the specific Client whose website transmitted the Client User Data to Sandstorm Analytics.

Sandstorm Analytics does not collect or aggregate information about end user behavior across multiple, different websites that are operated by different Clients, nor does it use any Client User Data for purposes of behavioral advertising. However, Sandstorm Analytics may analyze Client User Data in the aggregate for purposes of internal research and/or to determine overall trends or metrics concerning how users are engaging with websites, and may report such general trends publicly, without disclosing any Client User Data.

Our Clients Use of Sandstorm Analytics. Sandstorm Analytics contractually requires its Clients to abide by all applicable laws, rules and regulations, including Privacy Shield where it applies, as well as other applicable laws relating to privacy and data collection (Applicable Laws). Sandstorm Analytics also contractually requires its Clients to obtain any necessary consents from visitors to its website(s) for the Clients use of Sandstorm Analytics services, comply with all Applicable Laws (including, as an example, Privacy Shield where it applies) with respect to the transfer of Personally Identifiable Information of EU or Swiss subjects through the Service, and to post an online privacy policy that provides each visitor with clear notice of its practices regarding data collection, use, and disclosure (including the Clients use of Sandstorm Analytics), along with a link to the Sandstorm Analytics Privacy Policy and to the Sandstorm Analytics Service Opt-Out (discussed below).

Sandstorm Analytics cannot be held responsible for any Clients failure to make these disclosures on its website or for any Clients use or collection of Client User Data that is not permitted under this Privacy Policy or otherwise. For more details on the terms that apply to our Clients use of the Sandstorm Analytics Service, please see Section 4 (Privacy) in the Sandstorm Analytics  Terms of Use.


Sandstorm Analytics Use of Cookies and Web Beacons

Cookies. Sandstorm Analytics uses cookies (small text files that Sandstorm Analytics stores locally on your computer) on our Clients websites for one or more of the following purposes: to help identify unique visitors and/or devices; assess usage patterns and perform traffic analysis; identify visitor preferences; conduct A/B testing; diagnose problems with our servers, and otherwise administer the Sandstorm Analytics Service. Sandstorm Analytics does not use methods such as browser cache, Flash cookies, or eTags, for acquiring or storing information about end users web browsing activity on Client websites or otherwise.

Depending on how our Clients use Sandstorm Analytics Services, information contained in Sandstorm Analytics cookies placed on the computers of end users may be linked to Personally Identifiable Information in Sandstorm Analytics database. This allows our Clients to use the Sandstorm Analytics Service to better analyze and measure their users interactions with their website and to organize activities by the same user on their website across time.

If you do not wish to have Sandstorm Analytics place and use cookies on your computer, you should set your browser preferences to refuse all cookies before accessing Sandstorm Analytics or other websites that may use Sandstorm Analytics. Sandstorm Analytics is not responsible for any failure by you or your browser to accurately implement or communicate your browser preferences or settings. In addition, please be aware that even if you configure your browser settings to reject all cookies, your activity on Sandstorm Analytics client websites will still be recorded by our Service, unless you opt out of our Service as set forth below. However, your rejection of all cookies will prevent Sandstorm Analytics from determining whether or not someone using your computer has previously visited the website, i.e., whether or not the visitor is a unique visitor, and from retrieving any information about your prior activities on a Clients website through information contained in a cookie.

Please note that if you set your browser to reject cookies, some features of the websites you visit may be unavailable. For more information on how to reject cookies, see your browser’s instructions on changing your cookie settings. Additionally, please note that rejecting cookies may also prevent you from opting out of our Service because that involves placing an opt-out cookie in your browser.

If you do not elect to set your browser preferences to refuse cookies, you consent to have cookies, including cookies used by Sandstorm Analytics, placed on your computer when you visit our Clients websites.

Web Beacons. Sandstorm Analytics may also collect certain information using Web Beacons. Web Beacons are electronic images that may be used on Sandstorm Analytics Website, in Sandstorm Analytics Service, or in our email communications. They are used, for example, to count website visits or to tell if an email has been opened and acted upon.

User Privacy Controls and Do Not Track Signals

Clients and Visitors to the Sandstorm Analytics Website. Sandstorm Analytics offers several ways for you to review and update account information that is obtained and stored by Sandstorm Analytics, or to change your Sandstorm Analytics communication preferences:

  • If you have a Sandstorm Analytics account, you can review or update your account information by contacting us at support@kissmetrics.io.
  • You may unsubscribe to any of our e-mail or direct message communications regarding updates or products by following the unsubscribe instructions in the body of any message.
  • You may contact us at privacy@kissmetrics.io, attn: Sandstorm Analytics Privacy Office.

Do Not Track Signals. Sandstorm Analytics Clients decide whether their use of Sandstorm Analytics Service is responsive to Do Not Track signals from browsers, and whether Sandstorm Analytics will be notified of a Do Not Track signal received from a users browser. If a Sandstorm Analytics Client enables the forwarding of a Do Not Track signal to Sandstorm Analytics, Sandstorm Analytics will attempt to read the signal and will not collect any Client User Data from a user visiting that Sandstorm Analytics Clients website during that session, following receipt of the Do Not Track signal. The Do Not Track signal is browser-specific, and will not work for mobile devices. If a Sandstorm Analytics Client does not forward Do Not Track signals to Sandstorm Analytics, then Sandstorm Analytics will not have any access to such signals from the Clients users, and such signal will have no effect on Sandstorm Analytics collection or use of data received from that user during the applicable session(s).
