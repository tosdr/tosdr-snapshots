PERSONAL INFORMATION WE COLLECT

When you visit the Site, we automatically collect certain information about your device, including information about your web browser, IP address, time zone, and some of the cookies that are installed on your device. Additionally, as you browse the Site, we collect information about the individual web pages or products that you view, what websites or search terms referred you to the Site, and information about how you interact with the Site. We refer to this automatically-collected information as Device Information.

We collect Device Information using the following technologies:

  • Cookies, which are data files containing identifiers (strings of letters and numbers) that are sent by a web server to a web browser and stored by the browser. The identifiers are then sent back to the server each time the browser requests a page from the server. Cookies may be either "persistent" cookies or "session" cookies. A persistent cookie will be stored by a web browser and will remain valid until its set expiry date, unless deleted by the user before the expiry date; a session cookie will expire at the end of the user session, when the web browser is closed. Cookies do not typically contain any information that personally identifies a user, but Personal Information that we store about you may be linked to the information stored in and obtained from cookies. For more information about cookies, including how to disable cookies, visit http://www.allaboutcookies.org.
  • Log files, which track actions occurring on the Site and collect data, including your IP address, browser type, Internet service provider, geolocation information, referring/exit pages, and date/time stamps.
  • Web beacons, tags, and pixels, which are electronic files used to record information about how you browse the Site.

Additionally when you make a purchase or attempt to make a purchase through the Site, we collect certain information from you, including your name, billing address, shipping address, payment information (including credit card numbers), email address, and phone number. We refer to this information as Order Information.

When we talk about Personal Information in this Privacy Policy, we are talking both about Device Information and Order Information.

SCHOOL ACCOUNTS

While we operate our site as a general audience website that does not knowingly collect information from children under 13, we make access to our site available to K-12 students and schools. Therefore, we provide this Childrens Privacy Policy to explain our privacy practices with respect to children under 13 who access and use our services, and through this Privacy Policy, we provide each school with all the notices required under the Childrens Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA).

Access to our service is only available upon request by a school and only for the purposes of performing services on behalf of the school. Schools that provide access to our service acknowledge that they provide any disclosures and receive any consents necessary for us to collect Personal Information directly from students (Student Personal Information) under 13 in accordance with the "school consent" mechanism allowed under COPPA.

To prevent unauthorized collection of Student Personal Information, students under 13 cannot independently access our services without access to an account from the schools that request our services.

Student Personal Information may include:

  • Student login ID and password
  • First and last name;
  • The class and school where a student is enrolled;
  • Student-generated content, such as responses to questions, prompts and assignments;
  • Data generated from student activity within the site, including answers provided, errors, hint requests, preferences, timing information, progress within the system, and inferences made from the this data about student knowledge;
  • Feedback about any of our products and services; and
  • Assessment scores used to customize instruction.

We do not enable children under 13 to make their Personal Information publicly available.

In addition, we may automatically collect information about the devices children under 13 use to interact with our services. The information we automatically collect may include IP address, device identifier, web browser type and version, pages and activities viewed, and the date and time of use. Any usage information, device identifier, or other persistent identifier we collect is used for the sole purpose of providing support for internal operations.

Our collection, use, and disclosure of Personal Student Information is governed by, and in compliance with, the provisions of the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), the Protection of Pupil Rights Amendment (PPRA), COPPA, and applicable state laws which relate to the collection and protection of Personal Student Information and educational records. We access and process Student Personal Information for the purposes of providing an outsourced institutional function pursuant to FERPA 34 CFR Part 99.31(a)(1). In offering access to our site, we will be considered a school official with a legitimate educational interest in the Student Personal Information, under the direction and control of school.

All Student Personal Information collected, generated or received by us is and will continue to be the property of and under the control of the relevant school or person who provided such data (such as the student or parent/guardian).

We collect this Student Personal Information about children under 13 for the use and benefit of teachers or school or school district administrators, and for no other commercial purpose. We limit the use of Student Personal Information about children under 13 to the educational context authorized by the school. We will not use any Student Personal Information collected about children under 13 to advertise or market to students or their parents. We will never condition a students participation in our service on the childs disclosure of more Student Personal Information than is necessary to participate in the activity.

Uses of Student Personal Information collected from children under 13 include:

  • Personalizing the teaching and learning resources to improve student instruction and meet individual student needs;
  • Providing reports to teachers or school or school district administrators at the student, class, school, or district level;
  • Supporting teachers or schools or school district administrators implementing our services;
  • Monitoring the use of our services and performing such analyses as might be necessary or helpful in improving product performance, efficiency, and security;
  • Researching how students use our services and performing analysis directed at improving the educational effectiveness of our services;
  • Providing reports to students, parents or guardians; and
  • Helping students reset their passwords.

We may rely on third party service providers who provide support for the internal operations of our services, such as hosting the website, designing and/or operating our digital properties features, tracking analytics, or performing other administrative services. These third party service providers are held to the same obligations we are with regard to Student Personal Information from children under 13.

We do not permit third party advertising networks to collect information from Student Accounts about your use of our service over time and across websites for the purpose of serving targeted advertising, and we will never use Student Personal Information for targeted advertising.

We do not rent or sell Student Personal Information for marketing purposes, and we will never share or sell Student Personal Information with third parties for marketing purposes.

We may share information in an aggregated and/or anonymous form that does not reasonably identify an individual or school. For example, we may use and share aggregate or anonymized data for analytics purposes and to study and improve our service and user functionality. In addition, for research and other purposes, we may transfer de-identified data to third parties. For example, we may share information such as ratings and demand.

We acknowledge the rights of parents and legal guardians to review any educational data pertaining to their children under FERPA, and you always remain in control of the information you provide to us. To request information pertaining to a student or for any questions related to Student Personal Information, you must contact your childs school. We promptly route requests made by parents or legal guardians to the relevant school, and we will assist the school as needed to provide parents and legal guardians access to the requested information within a reasonable time frame.
