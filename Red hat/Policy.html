Prohibited Use: Unauthorized recording, copying, or transmission of Open Enrollment content is strictly prohibited.

Client will not be eligible for a refund of paid registration fees if Red Hat does not receive a notice of cancellation or rescheduling at least fourteen (14) calendar days prior to the class/exam start date.

Substitution of Enrollees: Client may substitute enrollees as long as the substitution is made no fewer than seven (7) calendar days prior to the class start date for classes or the exam date for exams. It is the responsibility of Client to ensure that the substitute enrollee meets the stated course prerequisites and is provided with any course materials already provided to enrollees.

Red Hat Certification and Preliminary Exams General Terms

These Red Hat exam General Terms apply to all Red Hat Certification and Preliminary Exams.

Certification: Granting of Red Hat certified status is at the sole discretion of Red Hat. Red Hat certified professionals are expected to abide by all applicable Red Hat exam policies, applicable Red Hat Exam Confidentiality Agreements, and testing guidance and procedures. Red Hat certified professionals should provide truthful documentation regarding their identity. To protect the integrity of the Red Hat exams and certifications, Red Hat certified professionals are expected to maintain at all times the highest standards of integrity and comply with all applicable laws and regulations. Any violation of these Training Policies or of the Red Hat Exam Confidentiality Agreement or Red Hat Exam Privacy and Confidentiality Agreement (in case of Red Hat Individual Exams and Preliminary Exams) may result in penalties provided for in the applicable Agreement and these Training Policies, including, but not limited to, nullification of exam results and revocation of exam pass status and certification.

Identification: All Named Participants must provide a current government-issued photo identification (ID) - preferably a valid passport (if available)- at the time of the exam.

  • Norwegian bank cards, commonly used as government ID equivalents for air travel and other critical identity checks may be used as identification for exams taken in Norway.
  • A Named Participant, who changes his or her name after registration, must provide legal documentation recognizing the change to be admitted to take the exam.
  • Under no circumstances may a Named Participant sit for a certification or expertise exam without providing positive photo identification (preferably a valid passport) as defined by these policies. Red Hat will expunge any certification or credential earned for Named Participants who have provided falsified information or who are found not to have provided positive identification for an exam.

  • Due to the increasing difficulty in identifying SMART devices, the following items may not be worn during an exam:

    • Any type of Headphones or Headsets
    • Earphones or Earbuds
  • Named Participants may not use or have out at any time during a Red Hat exam any digital materials or Smart or connected devices including but not limited to: Smart phones, tablets or other similar Smart devices.
  • Named Participants must turn off and unplug any other laptops, computers, displays, or any other personal equipment or hardware. Access to any systems or networks is not allowed when the device is being used for taking a Red Hat exam.

    • For Red Hat Individual Exams, an extended amount of time to complete the exam can be provided.
    • For Red Hat Classroom Exams, special accommodations may include a separate room, larger monitors, additional time to complete the exams, as well as other potential accommodations.

Red Hat Individual Exams

Red Hat Individual Exams (also known here as "Individual Exams") are certification exams administered via specially configured testing systems. Individual Exams can be delivered at a Red Hat testing center or online as a Remote Exam.

Individual Exam Scheduling: Named Participants have one (1) year from date of purchase to schedule and take the exam. No extensions of the one (1) year term will be granted. A Named Participant must schedule their exam start time no less than twenty-four (24) hours prior to their selected start date and time.

No Cancellation of Purchase: All Red Hat Individual Exam purchases are final, and fees are non-refundable.

Daily Limit: A Named Participant may not take the same exam more than once in the same day. Red Hat may at its sole discretion elect to enforce a minimum interval between exam attempts.

Rescheduling Individual Exams: Individual Exams can be rescheduled up to twenty-four (24) hours prior to the scheduled exam session. Any changes made to the start time of a scheduled exam less than twenty-four (24) hours prior to the scheduled start time will result in forfeiture of the Named Participant’s exam authorization.

No Exchange for Red Hat Classroom Exams: Red Hat does not permit exchanging an Individual Exam for a Classroom Exam. In the event that a Named Participant chooses to change their scheduled Individual Exam to a Red Hat Classroom Exam, they will be required to cancel their Individual Exam and book a new Red Hat Classroom Exam, thereby forfeiting their Individual Exam fees.

Substitution of Named Participants: Named Participant substitution for Individual Exam sessions is not allowed at any time.

Late Arrivals and No Show Policy: Failure to attend an exam, or arriving more than fifteen (15) minutes after the start time of an exam will result in forfeiture of the exam session and fee, and the Named Participant will not be eligible to reschedule the exam.

Country of Purchase: Unless otherwise approved by Red Hat in writing, all Individual Exams must be taken in the enrollee’s country of residence at the time of purchase. Named Participants are prohibited from taking their exams remotely from a country under embargo by the United States.

Red Hat Individual Exam Day Rules and Requirements: In addition to the terms set forth in the section Red Hat Certification and Preliminary Exam General Terms above, the terms below explain the additional rules and requirements on exam day for Red Hat Individual Exams.

  • Identity Authentication: A photo of the Named Participant will be taken prior to the start of the exam and the Named Participant must present a current government issued photo identification (preferably a valid passport). Government issued identification that uses non-Latin characters must be verified by a local Red Hat representative at the testing location or the online proctor in the case of Red Hat Remote Exams prior to the Named Participant taking a Red Hat exam.
  • Individual Exam Monitoring: By taking a Red Hat Individual Exam Named Participant will be monitored in real time by a combination of a human proctor and Artificial Intelligence (AI) to detect cheating or content theft during exams in order to protect Red Hat’s legitimate interest in the integrity of its testing and certification processes. Both AI and human proctors will monitor video and audio streams of Named Participant while taking the exam to detect behaviors that might indicate misconduct, such as the presence of another person in the room or eye movements that suggest Named Participant might be consulting notes or other prohibited materials. AI will only be used for the purpose of providing an alert to human proctors that a behavior has been observed that might indicate misconduct, but AI will not take actions or make decisions about Named Participant’s exam session. These will only be made by one or more remote human proctors.
  • Communication with the proctor is available in several languages. The proctor will guide Named Participant through the onboarding process using live video.
