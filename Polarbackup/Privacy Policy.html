How It Works

Polar Backup Products save a copy of each file that is automatically selected for backup and other files you designate for backup (your “Backed-up Data”) to a server operated by Amazon. Polar Backup Products automatically scan for changes or additions to the Backed-up Data and then periodically re-save a copy or a part of a modified file or create a copy of a newly designated file. For a complete list of the files Polar Backup is backing-up for you, please refer to the Polar Backup web console. You can check whether Polar Backup is backing-up specific files by going to Polar Backup Timeline located in your Computer, Web console, or from your mobile. If you are unable to locate your file, Polar Backup is not backing-up that file. Your Backed-up Data may not be available or restorable if: (i) Polar Backup has not completed copying your selected files or changed files; (ii) for files, folders, or disk drives that are not automatically backed-up, you do not manually select them for backup, or you unselect a file for backup; (iii) you delete a person from your Polar Backup account; (iv) you move a file to a location on your computer that is not automatically scanned to select files for backup or you upgrade your operating system resulting in changes to your file mapping; (v) your computer is unable to access the internet or to Amazon s3 Storage; (vi) you fail to follow Polar Backup technical requirements, including upgrading the version of your Polar Backup Products as required; or (vii) you terminate your license or fail to renew your subscription to Polar Backup Products.
