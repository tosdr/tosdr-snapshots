COOKIES AND OTHER WEB SITE USAGE MONITORING

We use cookies on our website. In order for the pages and services to work properly, you should allow cookies. Below you will find out more about the purposes for which cookies are collected and how you can influence the management of cookies.

WHAT ARE COOKIES?

Cookies allow websites to track your interest in the items on which websites you have visited and what you have viewed on the site. In practice, cookies are small text files that your web browser stores on your device. These cookies contain a unique identifier that identifies your browser. Information is collected on the visited pages and cookies do not contain personal information.

SITE COOKIES

We use cookies to monitor and analyze the use of our pages. Through cookies we collect information about how many people use our services, how long they spend time on our site and what they are looking in our site. Cookies also tell us how you came to the School Day website and where you go from the website. Information is used to develop our products and services.

THIRD-PARTY COOKIES

School Day sites use also third-party cookies, which means in practice that the School Day partners may set cookies on your computer when you visit our company website. We obtain from these service providers, for example, reporting and analytics tools that provide information we use in our own operations. Our services also include so-called community links such as Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn and Instagram. If you have allowed the use of cookies in community-based services, it is possible that these providers will collect similar information about your actions on the School Day website.

BLOCKING COOKIES AND BLOCKING THEM

You can actually influence what cookies you want to accept. In practice, you can do this through your own browser settings. You can block cookies completely, but you may not be able to use all pages and services as intended. However, cookies can be removed at regular intervals, and by doing this it is not possible to create a long-term user profile when website needs to start creating it from the beginning in the absence of baseline data. The third option is to limit the use of cookies by excluding third-party cookies from the allowed list.
