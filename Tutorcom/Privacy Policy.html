The Princeton Review and Tutor.com Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 4, 2024

Tutor.com, Inc. and TPR Education, LLC d/b/a The Princeton Review (collectively, “we,” “us,” or “our”) take your privacy seriously and understand the importance of protecting your personal information. Therefore, this Privacy Policy provides information about our privacy practices. “Personal information” means, unless otherwise defined by applicable law, any information about, that identifies, that relates to, that is linked to or associated with, or that is reasonably linkable to or reasonably capable of being associated with an identified or identifiable individual. For clarity, this Privacy Policy does not apply to our employees, job applicants, and independent contractors.

By visiting this site and other websites and mobile applications which link to this Privacy Policy (collectively, “Sites”) or using the products or services offered through this Site, you agree to this Privacy Policy, as it may be amended from time to time. This Privacy Policy is incorporated into the Terms of Use for this Site.

Availability of Anonymous Access

Depending on the organization through which you access our online, on-demand tutoring programs, you can access the program through a guest account, an anonymous account, or a personalized account.

If you access the program through a guest account, we do not collect personal information such as your name or email address from you to create an account. Certain features of the program will not be available to you, such as voice or videos sessions, essay drop off review, or storage of past sessions.

If you access the program through an anonymous account, then the email address you used to create an individual account is only stored in our system as an un-recognizable “hash” of letters and numbers, using an irreversible encryption algorithm. Therefore, you will not receive email communication of any kind from us, and certain features of the program will not be available to you, such as voice or videos sessions and essay drop off review.

The benefit to a personalized account is to make available certain features of the service like:

  • Making your sessions personal;
  • Access to voice and, when generally available, video sessions, and essay drop off review;
  • Storing all of your past sessions, so you can review them anytime; and
  • Access to our mobile application.

Children under the Age of 13

Depending on the organization through which you access our program, you use our program through a guest account (as described above), an anonymous account (as described above), or a personalized account with the permission of your parent or legal guardian (obtained by your school or provided by your school on behalf of your parent or legal guardian if you are using our program through your school or obtained by us) for us to collect, use, or disclose your personal information in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

Note to parents of children under the age of 13: You are welcome to contact us at (800) 411-1970 to review any personal information we have collected about your child. You may request that we delete this information or specify that we collect no more of your child's information. We will need to verify the identity of anyone requesting information about a child under the age of 13 to ensure that the person is in fact the child' parent or legal guardian.

We may collect personal and non-personal information about you (such as your name, email address, username, student ID number, school name, grade level, and courses you are taking) from third parties with whom we have a business relationship (such as our organizational customers and resellers).

Information Collected Automatically.

We collect information (such as your IP address, machine ID, your browser type, cookies, the referring website address, and operating system) from your browser when you visit our Site.

We, our service providers and other vendors, and our affiliates and third parties which may display advertisement on our Site may use tracking technologies such as cookies to collect information about your online interactions with our Site and our emails.

We collect information when you use our products and services (such as when we record online sessions and produce transcripts of online tutoring sessions).

We automatically record calls to our call center.
