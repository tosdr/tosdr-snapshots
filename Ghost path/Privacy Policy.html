Privacy Policy

Last updated: August 1, 2019

1. Information We Collect

We only collect personal information that is relevant to the purpose of our website, service, and billing. This information allows us to provide you with a customized and efficient experience. We collect the following types of information from customers: Name, Email Address, Credit Card and Other Billing Information

All billing information that we receive from our customers is stored securely with our billing processor. We do not store credit card information locally.

Certain information, such as your IP address and the pages that you visit on the Site are available to us automatically (see the section on Cookies and Tracking for more information). We do not track activities outside of the Ghost Path site.

Summary

We need some basic information to create your account and bill you.

2. Information We Collect About A VPN Session

We do not log any data related to your VPN sessions.

Summary

No logs, ever.

3. Email

Ghost Path will never send marketing email to you unless you specifically opt-in to receive those communications. We will only send you transactional emails, such as welcome emails, payment receipts, etc.

If you do opt-in to marketing emails then we will always provide you with an easy way to unsubscribe in each marketing email that we send you.

We will never sell or rent your email address to a third-party.

Summary

No spam, ever.

4. Sharing Your Information

Ghost Path will neither rent nor sell your personal information to anyone and we will share your personal information only as described below.

Ghost Path Personnel: Ghost Path personnel and authorized consultants and/or contractors may have access to user information if necessary in the normal course of Ghost Path business.

Service Providers: Ghost Path may use third-party service providers, including but not limited to payment processors and email marketing platforms. We will only share the minimum amount of data needed for the third-party to perform their designated functions.

Business Transfers: In some cases, we may choose to buy or sell assets. In these types of transactions, user information is typically one of the business assets that is transferred. Moreover, if Ghost Path, or substantially all of its assets, were acquired, user information would be one of the assets that is transferred.

Protection of Ghost Path and Others: We may release personal information when we believe in good faith that release is necessary to comply with a law; to enforce or apply our Terms of Service and other policies; or to protect the rights, property, or safety of Ghost Path, our employees, our users, or others. This includes exchanging information with other companies and organizations for fraud protection and credit risk reduction.

Summary

We keep your information private.

5. Keeping Personal Information Secure

Your Ghost Path account is password protected. We encrypt passwords before storing them to maximize security. You should also make sure that you keep your password secure.

Ghost Path employees, consultants and contractors may only access your personal information if needed for a specific reason, such as a customer support request. Ghost Path team members, consultants and contractors are trained in our privacy policies and understand that privacy is the top priority.

Summary

Don't let others know your password.

6. How To Contact Ghost Path

Ghost Path Support is always available. Please send an email to help@ghostpath.com if you have any questions or concerns regarding this Privacy policy.

Summary

Email help@ghostpath.com if you have questions.

7. Cookies

Cookies are alphanumeric identifiers that are stored on your device through your browser to enable our systems to recognize your browser and tell us how and when pages in our website are visited and by how many people. Ghost Path cookies do not collect personal information, and we do not combine information collected through cookies with other personal information to tell us who you are.

The "help" portion of the toolbar on the majority of browsers will direct you on how to prevent your browser from accepting new cookies, how to command the browser to tell you when you receive a new cookie, or how to fully disable cookies. We recommend that you leave the cookies activated.

Summary

We need cookies to be able to log you into our site.
