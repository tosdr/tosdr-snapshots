PRIVACY POLICY

OVERVIEW

This Privacy Policy explains The Citizenry’s online and offline information practices, the kinds of information we may collect, how we intend to use and share that information, and how you can opt out of, correct, or change such information.

By using or accessing The Citizenry’s Services in any manner, you acknowledge that you accept the practices and policies outlined in our Privacy Policy, and you hereby consent that we will collect, use and share your information as detailed below. If you are a resident of California, Nevada, or the European Union (“EU”), United Kingdom, Lichtenstein, Norway, or Iceland, you may have additional rights with respect to your personal information as outlined below.

Your use of The Citizenry’s Services is governed by our Terms, which incorporates this Privacy Policy. Terms used in this Policy without definition use the definitions given in the Terms. If you have a disability and would like to access this privacy policy in an alternate format, please contact assistant@the-citizenry.com.

SCOPE

This Privacy Policy describes how your personal information is collected, used, and shared when you visit or make a purchase from www.the-citizenry.com (the “Site”). This Privacy Policy does not apply to any third-party websites, services or applications, even if they are accessible through our Site. “Personal Information” means any information that identifies or relates to a particular individual and also includes information referred to as “personally identifiable information” or “personal information” under applicable data privacy laws, rules, or regulations. This Policy applies only to Personal Data collected directly from you; if we did not collect data directly from you, you should contact the entity that collected your Personal Data in the first instance to address your rights with respect to this data.

We gather various types of Personal Information from our users, as detailed below. This information is used in connection with our services including to contact you, to allow you set up and manage a user account, to fulfill your order, to personalize your experience, and to analyze how you use our Services. Your Personal Information may be shared with third parties, but only in the capacity described below.

All individuals whose responsibilities include the Processing of Personal Information on behalf of The Citizenry are expected to protect that data by adherence to this Privacy Policy. This Privacy Policy is intended to meet requirements globally, including those in North America, Europe, APAC, and other jurisdictions.

PERSONAL INFORMATION WE COLLECT

The following section details the categories of Personal Information we have collected over the last twelve (12) months.

Information You Provide to Us

The Citizenry retains personal information that you share through the following types of interactions:

Placing an Order with Us. When you make a purchase or attempt to make a purchase through the Site, you provide certain types of information directly to us, including your name, billing address, shipping address, payment information (including credit card numbers and PayPal or ApplePay account information), email address, and phone number. We refer to this information as “Order Information”.

Creating an Account with Us. You have the option of creating an account with The Citizenry to allow easy access to your order history. During account creation, you provide your name and email address directly to us.

Communicating with Us. We may collect personal information such as phone number, email address or mailing address when you request information about The Citizenry, register for The Citizenry’s newsletter, request customer support, or otherwise communicate with us.

Completing Surveys. Occasionally, we may ask you to participate in online surveys about our products or services. If you decide to participate, you may be asked to provide certain personal information. All of this information is provided by you voluntarily, and may be used to improve our products, Site, or services.

Posting on the Site. You may add blog comments or post reviews of your purchases on The Citizenry’s site. When you disclose personal information through one of these submissions, it will be collected by the Site. If you choose to submit content to any public area of the Site, such content will be considered “public” and not subject to the privacy protections in this policy.

Registering for Sweepstakes or Contests. Occasionally, The Citizenry may run sweepstakes and contests. We ask those who enter in the sweepstakes or contests to provide contact information (e.g., an e-mail address). If you participate in a sweepstakes or contest, your contact information may be used to reach you about the sweepstakes or contest, and for other promotional, marketing and business purposes. 

Information Collected Automatically or From Others

Automatic Data Collection. When you visit the Site, some information is automatically collected, including information about your web browser, IP address, time zone, and some of the cookies installed on your device. Additionally, as you browse the Site, we collect information about the individual web pages or products that you view, what websites or search terms referred you to the Site, and information about how you interact with the Site. We refer to this automatically-collected information as “Device Information”. This is used to personalize your site experience and to tailor content to you (on The Citizenry’s website and in advertising).

Cookies, Pixel Tags/Web Beacons, and Interest-Based Advertising. We, as well as third parties that provide content, advertising, or other functionality on the Services, may use cookies, pixel tags, local storage, and other technologies (“Technologies”) to automatically collect information through the Services. Technologies are essentially small data files placed on your computer, tablet, mobile phone, or other devices that allow us and our partners to record certain pieces of information whenever you visit or interact with our Services.

Cookies. Cookies are small text files placed in visitors’ computer browsers to store their preferences. Most browsers allow you to block and delete cookies. However, if you do that, the Services may not work properly.

Pixel Tags/Web Beacons. A pixel tag (also known as a web beacon) is a piece of code embedded in the Services that collects information about users’ engagement on that web page. The use of a pixel allows us to record, for example, that a user has visited a particular web page or clicked on a particular advertisement.

Analytics We also use Google Analytics, Hotjar, and other service providers to collect information regarding visitor behavior and visitor demographics on our Services. For more information about Google Analytics, please visit www.google.com/policies/privacy/partners/. You can opt out of Google’s collection and processing of data generated by your use of the Services by going to http://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.

Exercising Your Rights

To exercise the rights described above, you must submit a request 1) via a verified email address that confirms you are the person about whom we have collected Personal Data and 2) that describes your request in sufficient detail to allow us to understand, evaluate, and respond to it. We will only use Personal Data provided in a Valid Request to verify you and complete your request. You do not need an account to submit a Valid Request.

We will work to respond to your Valid Request within 45 days of receipt. We will not charge you a fee for making a Valid Request unless your Valid Request(s) is excessive, repetitive, or manifestly unfounded. If we determine that your Valid Request warrants a fee, we will notify you of the fee and explain that decision before completing your request.

You may submit a Valid Request using the following methods:
